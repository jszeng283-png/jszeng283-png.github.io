About

Academic Profile

Prof. Zeng is an Associate Professor at the Faculty of Law, The University of Hong Kong. He specializes in corporate law, the Chinese legal system, and empirical legal studies. His research explores the intersection of law, economics, and organizational theory, with particular focus on ownership structures, regulatory design, and comparative corporate governance.

Previous Positions

Prior to joining HKU, Prof. Zeng served as a tenured Associate Professor of Law, Convenor of the Faculty Seminar Series, Deputy Executive Director of the Center for Comparative and Transnational Law, and Deputy Director of the LLM Program at the Faculty of Law, Chinese University of Hong Kong.

Research Focus

State Ownership & Regulation

Examining how state ownership functions as a mechanism for regulation and the implications for corporate law and public policy in China and comparative contexts.

Shareholder Protection

Analyzing legal mechanisms for protecting minority shareholders, including defensive tactics, related-party transactions, and rules versus standards approaches.

Empirical Law & Economics

Applying quantitative methods to understand how legal institutions affect corporate behavior, market outcomes, and economic organization.

Comparative Corporate Law

Comparative analysis of corporate governance structures, ownership patterns, and legal frameworks across the United States, China, and other jurisdictions.

Environmental Law

Research on environmental policy, compensation mechanisms, and administrative license law with empirical analysis of real-world outcomes.

Financial Regulation

Study of securities regulation, financial holding companies, and the role of state authority in financial governance and market supervision.
